FAQ

What should I wear?

Wear something you feel comfortable in. Generally I massage direct to bare skin to get a better idea of whats going on in the tissue and be more effective, before the massage we will discuss the areas to be massaged and if you prefer to stay covered then treatment can be adapted to be done through clothing or towels. I will leave the room so that you can get undressed into what your comfortable in (usually underwear) and then lay on the table underneath a towel which is used to cover any areas that aren’t being worked on throughout the massage.

Should I come if I have an injury?

Massage can be great for healing injuries, reducing stiffness and restoring movement. If you have just been injured it’s recommended that you spend the first 72 hours in R-I-C-E mode (resting, icing, compressing & elevating the injury) to minimize inflammation and pain. Generally after the 72 hour mark the injury will start to enter the repair phase which is a good stage to start massage therapy.

What happens during the massage?

Before the massage we will discuss the areas to be massaged, and at any changes to plans you will be asked for consent before hand. I massage with oil or wax, any areas that are not being treated at the time will be coverred by a towel. You’re welcome to talk during the treatment, or if you just prefer to relax and breathe that’s fine too and may even help to make the massage more effective.

What is an initial consultation?

Every person has a different past and different needs. In our initial consultation we will spend 5 - 10 minutes discussing any injuries and understanding the current state of your body to figure out the ideal treatment plan for you. In follow up treatments we will generally only need 5 minutes to chat about your progression.

I’m nervous about pain, will it hurt?

It’s important that your comfortable, the pressure of the massage can be adjusted to your preference. I will often ask if the pressure is okay, and at any time during the massage you can ask for more or less pressure.

How often should I have a massage?

It varies from person to person, and depends on the goal of treatments. For acute pain I may recommend weekly massages at first, while some other cases I might recommend fortnightly or monthly massages.
